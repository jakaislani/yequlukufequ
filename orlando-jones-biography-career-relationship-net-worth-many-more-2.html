Orlando Jones: Biography, Career, Relationship, Net worth &amp; many more

Orlando Jones is a stand-up comic and actor from the United States. He is well-known for playing the African god Anansi on Starz’s American Gods and for serving as one of the original cast members of the sketch comedy series MADtv. From 1999 to 2002, he also served as the 7 Up ambassador.

Who Is Orlando Jones?

Jones was born in 1968 in Mobile, Alabama.  His father played baseball professionally for the Philadelphia Phillies.  As a teenager, he relocated to Mauldin, South Carolina, where he later earned his high school diploma in 1985. Playing a werewolf in a haunted home to earn money for the junior/senior prom was one of his earliest acting gigs. Jones registered at Charleston College in South Carolina. Without earning his degree, he quit in 1990.

Jones founded Homeboy’s Productions and Advertising with comedian Michael Fechter to further his passion for the entertainment business. Jones and Fechter collaborated on a number of projects, including a McDonald’s advertisement featuring basketball legend Michael Jordan promoting the “McJordan” specialty sandwich.

He wrote for the NBC comedy A Different World in 1987, landing his first Hollywood job. He also had a short cameo in the season five finale of the show. Jones wrote the Fox television series Roc in 1991–1992, and he co-produced The Sinbad Show in 1993. He also appeared briefly in the FOX comedy Herman’s Head in 1992.

 Jones has been a part of numerous voice-acting productions. He starred in Yuletide in the ‘hood in 1993, and in King of the Hill, an animated comedy TV series, he made a special appearance in 1998. Later, he provided voices for the computer games Halo 2 and another TV show, Father of the Pride, in which he played Marine Sergeant Banks.
